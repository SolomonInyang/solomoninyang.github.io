The Forest Digital Twin Project

An interactive digital twin developed in 12 weeks using environmental
data provided from a real Australian plantation.
built in collaboration with the Australian Plant Phenomics Facility.

Project Overview

Our aim for the forest digital twin was to build a virtual
model that can simulate the growth and development of trees over time. By integrating satellite image data, climate models and historical growth
data, the forest digital twin provides farmers and landowners with accurate
projections of how their forest will change over time.

Features

1. Advanced visualizations of key above and below-ground ecological processes.
2. Test and evaluate response options for different land-based challenges such as
climate change, biodiversity loss, and loss of food security.
3. Use gamification to make educating users on ecology and land management fun and engaging.

Accurate Ecological Modelling

The digital twin uses a point-based Stella simulation model developed by
Dr. Firouzeh Taghikhah to simulate various ecological
processes including crop growth, water flow, soil loss, and yield.

Realistic Lighting and Weather Simulation

The digital twin features dynamic lighting and
and shadows that change depending on what time
of the day it is. In addition to this, there are realistic weather
events that affect the amount of water each plot of land receives.

Interactive Land Management

Users can interact with the digital twin by adding or removing trees.
In subsequent versions of the platform we plan to dynamically modify the amount
of soil nutrients, sunlight, and water each tree based on
its position.

Potential Future Work

Here are a few additional features that we plan to add
as development continues.

  • Deeper Simulation

    We plan to implement additional simulation modules such as wind, sunlight, and infiltration. We also plan to integrate more
    interactions between the ecological processes. Additionally, we want to implement a variety of different farming practices such as cropping and tillage to allow land owners to evaluate the potential ramifications of these practices in a consequence free environment.

  • Livestock

    We hope to implement AI livestock as a feature that will allow users to see what effect grazing animals have on natural processes like crop growth and pasture quality and have them take these effects into consideration when managing their land.

  • Machine Learning

    The use of machine learning algorithms has been discussed as a possible addition. These algorithms will provide recommendations on the best way to achieve specific goals and implement certain management practices. These recommendations will be personalised for each area of land based on the topology and other features.

  • Gamification

    We plan to enhance the user experience by integrating the gamification mechanics such as user objectives. These objectives will function similarly to traditional video game quests and will generate certain productivity goals for users to achieve, such as maximising yield.
