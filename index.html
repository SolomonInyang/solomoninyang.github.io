Project Overview

Our aim for the forest digital twin was to build a virtual model that can simulate the growth and development of trees over time. By integrating satellite image data, climate models and historical crop growth data, the forest digital twin provides farmers and land owners with accurate projections of how their forest will change over time.

Accurate Ecological Modelling

The digital twin uses a point-based Stella simulation model developed by
Dr. Firouzeh Taghikhah to simulate various ecological processes including crop growth, water flow, soil loss, and yield.

Dynamic Lighting and Weather

The digital twin features dynamic lighting and
and shadows that change depending on the time
of the day. Additionally, there are realistic weather
events that affect the amount of water each plot of land receives.

Interactive Land Management

Users can interact with the digital twin by adding or removing trees. In subsequent versions of the platform we plan to dynamically modify the amount of soil nutrients, sunlight, and water each tree receives based on its position.

Future Work

Here are a few additional features that we plan to add
as development continues.

  • Deeper Simulation

    In future versions of the tool, we plan to implement additional ecological simulation modules such as wind, sunlight, and infiltration, in addition to agroforestry practices such as alley cropping that will allow land owners to evaluate the potential ramifications of these practices in a consequence free environment.

  • Livestock

    Agent-based livestock will be a future addition that will allow users to see what effect grazing animals have on natural processes like crop growth and pasture quality, allowing users to take these effects into consideration when managing their land.

  • Recommendation System

    An AI-powered recommendation system is a future milestone that will provide users with suggestions on the best way to achieve specific productivity goals and implement certain management practices. These recommendations will be personalised for each individual farm based on geographical features.

  • Gamification

    Gamification mechanics such as user objectives will be added in future versions to enhance user experience and engagement. These objectives will generate certain productivity goals for users to achieve such as maximising yield.
