Project Overview

The aim of the gamified farm design project is to create a tool
that will enable farm and land owners to test and evaluate different design
and management options in an ecologically accurate simulation of their farm.

Desired Outcomes

1. Visualise and measure key ecological processes.
2. Test and evaluate response options for different land-based challenges such as
climate change, biodiversity loss, and loss of food security.
3. Use gamification to make educating users on ecology and land management fun and engaging.

Accurate Ecological Modelling

The game uses point-based environmental models developed by
Dr. Firouzeh Taghikhah to simulate a number of different
natural processes including crop growth, water flow, soil loss, and yield.

Realistic Day/Night Cycle and Weather

The game features a realistic day/night cycle with dynamic
lighting and shadows that change depending on what time
of the day it is. In addition to this, there are realistic rain
events that affect the amount of water each cell receives.

Interactive Land Management

The game allows users to add and remove trees and crops on
specific cells. In later versions of the tool we plan for the amount
of soil nutrients, sunlight, and water a tree gets to change
depending on the tree's position.

Potential Future Work

Here are a few additional features that we plan to add
as development continues.

  • Deeper Simulation

    We plan to add more above and below ground processes to the game such as wind, sunlight, and infiltration. We also plan to create more
    interaction between processes. In addition to this, we want to implement a variety of different farming practices such as cropping and tillage to allow land owners to evaluate the potential ramifications of these practices in a consequence free environment.

  • Livestock

    We hope to implement AI livestock as a feature that will allow users to see what effect grazing animals have on natural processes like crop growth and pasture quality and have them take these effects into consideration when managing their land.

  • Machine Learning

    The use of machine learning algorithms has been discussed as a possible addition. These algorithms will provide recommendations on the best way to achieve specific goals and implement certain management practices. These recommendations will be personalised for each farm based on the topology and features of the land.

  • User Objectives

    To expand on the gamification concept, user objectives may be added. These objectives will function similar to traditional video game quests and will designate certain productivity goals for the player to achieve, such as maximising yield.
